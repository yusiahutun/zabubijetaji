Would YOU survive an alien invasion? New study finds which states' residents have the best chance

May 17, 2024
blog

An ominous spike in UFO sightings has captured America's imagination - but what state is the safest to live in if aliens decide to invade the earth? 

A new study has attempted to put the question to rest, with Virginia residents the most likely to survive an extraterrestrial visit. 

According to researchers with njcasino.com, the chances of staying alive are determined by factors including how much each state spends on defense, its population density, and cover from incoming attacks in caves and woodland. 

The number of UFO sightings per state were also included in the study, after a Pentagon chief recently admitted hundreds of flying saucers have already been spotted 'all over the world.'

America's recent obsession with alien invaders comes after intelligence officials have made a series of startling revelations, with even Barack Obama admitting he saw UFO footage he 'can't explain'. 

And if aliens were to make their arrival more invasive, those who want to live should make their way to Virginia to hedge their bets on survival.  

Thanks to its impressive military spending coupled with a small population size, the Easy Coast metro came out on top in the rankings with a score of 8.04 out of 10. 

Georgia and Massachusetts rounded out the podium, while New York's expansive NYPD force helped the Big Apple come in a close fourth. 

Alongside a high number of cops, New Yorkers also enjoy over 2,600 food and drink spots, which would be sure to help residents make it through an alien apocalypse. 

However, West Coast citizens are set to fare far worse, with Nevada registering the lowest survivability score at just 4.53 out of 10. 

One of the main reasons for the state's poor showing is its large number of UFO sightings, while it also has a lack of helpful terrain such as caves and forests. 

The wide open plains of the West Coast also saw states including New Mexico, Arizona, Utah and Idaho fall to the bottom of the pack.

The study comes amid heightened interest in extraterrestrial activity in the US, with Congress hosting several high-profile hearings confirming the existence of 'UAPs' - stood for unidentified aerial phenomenon. 

Earlier this month, a Pentagon chief admitted during NASA's first-ever public hearing that hundreds of UFOs have been spotted 'all over the world'. 

Physicist Dr. Sean Kirkpatrick, director of the Pentagon's All-domain Anomaly Resolution Office (AARO), said: 'We see these ('metallic orbs') all over the world, and we see these making very interesting apparent maneuvers.' 

And a top attorney involved in bringing UFO whistleblowers to Congress has sensationally claimed that a crashed vessel recovered by the US military 'distorted space-time' when it was picked up. 

Daniel Sheehan says he was told the mind-boggling tale by a whistleblower who allegedly took part in an illegally-undisclosed program retrieving crashed non-human spacecraft.
