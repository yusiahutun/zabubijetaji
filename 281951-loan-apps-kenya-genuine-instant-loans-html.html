1. Tala

Tala is among the most popular loan apps in Kenya without CRB. The app has massive downloads and good ratings from users. Apply once and borrow against your credit limit of up to KSH 50,000 as often as needed. Download the Tala app from the Google Play Store and register to access loans.

The app verifies your details during the registration process before depositing the loan to your M-pesa account. You can repay the loan once or in installments using Mpesa Xpress or the Paybill number 851900.

2. Mshwari loans on Safaricom's M-pesa app

M-pesa Africa was incorporated in April 2020 and operates in 7 countries, including Kenya (Safaricom). Safaricom's M-pesa app is among Kenya's most widely used mobile loan services.

The platform's Mshwari mobile banking service has savings and credit services. You can only access M-Shwari loans (Ksh. 1,000 to Ksh. 1 million) if you have a registered Safaricom line. Your credit history determines your maximum limit. The loans have a 9% annual interest rate, a 7.5% one-time fee, and 1.5% excise duty fee.

3. Fuliza loans on Safaricom's M-pesa app

M-Shwari loans are slightly different from Safaricom's Fuliza. The latter is purely for credit services, while the former offers savings and credit options. You must repay a Fuliza loan within 30 days from the loan disbursement date. Additionally, interest and fees are added onto the Fuliza loans and deducted from your M-pesa account.

4. KCB mobile app

The Kenya Commercial Bank has a mobile banking platform called the KCB mobile app. The app provides an easy and secure way to access your KCB account, send money, withdraw cash, and access loans. The KCB mobile app is independent of the bank's other mobile money service, the KCB M-pesa, which is affiliated with Safaricom.

The Branch credit platform is among loan apps in Kenya without CRB checks. Registration is free within and outside the country. One can access personal loans ranging from Ksh. 500 to Ksh 300,000 on the Branch app.
