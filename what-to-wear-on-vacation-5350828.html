What to Wear on Vacation: 15 Pieces to Pack

April 11, 2024
blog

Vacations are supposed to be stress-free, and there is one surefire way to keep your next vacation relaxed before you leave your house. We're talking about packing the right pieces to ensure your vacation wardrobe is on point and easy to carry, especially if you're limited to just a carry-on.

Thankfully, celebrity stylists know a thing or two about packing light and packing right, and they've weighed in on the 15 pieces you should always bring along for a flawless traveling wardrobe. Together, their picks create the ultimate vacation capsule wardrobe that allows you to save room in your suitcase while still having many day-to-day options to choose from, no matter how long your stay.

A White Tee

When packing for vacation, celebrity stylist Tara Swennen suggests choosing neutral colors for the base of your looks, and a cropped or traditional white tee is a perfect example. "These pieces can be layered as much as possible and go with just about anything: knit pencil skirts, drawstring linen pants, overalls, high-waisted jeans... the list goes on and on," Swennen says. "They are truly the most versatile and lightweight piece to bring!"

Cropped Trousers

Contrary to popular belief, you don't have to pack bottoms of every shape and color when going on vacation. To keep things simple, Swennen says she likes to stick with three pairs of pants in three basic colors so that she can mix and match them with other neutral or fun pattern tops throughout her stay. "A black, white, and neutral pair will carry to just about anything and can be styled day to night by simply swapping a thing to a heel," she tells us. "I like linens, crepes, or a stretchy fabric that doesn't wrinkle easily and therefore can carry you through a few occasions!"

A Bucket Hat

Give your vacation wardrobe a bit of throwback energy starting with the bucket hatâ€”a.k.a., the perfect accessory to block out the sun or to add a bit of panache to your polo shirt and trousers, according to fashion stylist Mickey Freeman. "Bucket hats are giving men and women of all ages and walks of life shade while keeping them 'cool,'" he tells InStyle.

A Multi-Faceted Mini Skirt

Raise your hand if you've ever worn a mini skirt as a shirt. If not, celebrity stylist Britt Theodora highly recommends giving this (brilliant) hack a try. "I love two-in-one pieces especially if you are packing a carry-on," Theodora says. "I would style [a skirt] with linen high-waisted pants if I were wearing it as a shirt. If wearing it as a skirt, I would style it with a tie-at-the-tummy button-up shirt!"

Blue Jeans

Blue jeans and white tees go together like pool bars and bay breezes, but Swennen says that a pair of A+ jeans will get far more than one use on your vacation. "Blue jeans are extremely versatile. Wear them with a loose-fitting blouse, a T-shirt, a duster for an early evening stroll, or with a sexy, tight, knit top for a night on the town," she says.

Cutoff Shorts

While we're on the subject of denim, cutoff shorts are another great example of a piece that can easily be dressed up or down. To do this, Freeman suggests using cool accessories like stacked bracelets or necklaces to add a personal touch. Don't be afraid to rock cut-offs with a pair of wedges or heeled sandals and be sure to have fun with accessories. You are on vacation, after all.

A Sundress

By simply swapping flip-flops for a pair of sexy strappy heels or even some coastal cowgirl-inspired boots, Swennen says that sundresses can be your perfect done-in-one day-to-night piece for a summer vacation. "Pair with a belted blazer, layer with a fun sweater or wrap for the evening, or keep it casual wedges and a fun tote! It's a summer must!"

A Linen Button-Down

Button-downs make for great tops, but they also make for great layering pieces as well. Theodora says she loves throwing an oversized men's fit "over bathing suits, with jean shorts or unbuttoned."

A Statement Accessory

While you may not need to stuff five pairs of shoes into your suitcase, according to Swennen, a statement accessory is something you might consider adding in. "This could be as simple as a vintage chain Chanel belt or an amazing collar necklace," she tells us. "This will be the one thing that helps elevate your daytime look to a fun evening occasion."

Don't feel comfortable traveling with the family jewels? Opt for a statement headband instead.

Rope Sandals

Speaking of footwear, as far as Theodora is concerned, rope sandals should be a staple in your vacation wardrobe. "I love styling pops of colors with my outfits and these shoes are great for mixing it up!"

A Swim Coverup

The one piece to pack for any warm-weather vacation would have to be a coverup, says Aerie director of styling Erin Collins Rittling, who suggests a colorful option as the perfect pick. "Designed to take you to the beach and beyond, the right coverup is perfect to throw over a swimsuit during the day or can be dressed up for a night out by adding a touch of jewelry or embellished headband."

A Wicker Tote

According to celebrity stylist Tyler Minor wicker totes are the It vacation bag because they're so versatile. "These handbags are great to hold your towel and SPF at the beach, or throw a fun scarf around the handle and take it to a fabulous lunch."

Birkenstock Sandals

Love them or hate them, Birkenstocks are back for good, and Freeman is vouching for them as a vacation must-have. "Now available in new and fun monochromatic colorways, this lightweight and comfortable sandal is a perfect way to balance out a sexy swimsuit with a semi-sheer cover-up to achieve comfortable sexiness," he says.

Your Favorite Sunglasses

Don't forget your sunnies! While any pair and style will do (whatever makes you feel the best), Freeman tells InStyle he gravitates towards aviators "because they provide maximum coverage, which protects you against those unrelenting sun rays and can also bring a hint of masculinity to the colorful looks people tend to gravitate towards while on vacation."

A Colorful Kimono

As far as this writer is concerned, Kimono-like styles aren't talked about nearly enough in the fashion realm, and Minor concurs. "Kimonos are great for summer vacations," he tells InStyle. "They can be worn at the pool or you can pair them with a bralette and some cut-off denim shorts for a day of exploring."
