Kendall Jenner makes the case for cowboy boots

March 30, 2024
blog

Kendall Jenner gave us a dose of street style inspiration this week when she was pictured out in Los Angeles wearing two classic fashion staples - the slip dress and the cowboy boot.

In a post-pandemic world, when shopping for timeless, versatile items is top of the agenda, Jenner's look is a perfect example of how to style simple, easy-to-wear pieces that won't ever date.

Of course, the cowboy boot is a style which rears its head every few seasons, whether in the sleek, angular fashion of Phoebe Philo's Céline boots for spring/summer 2016 or Raf Simons' take for Calvin Klein in autumn/winter 2017. They were also particularly ubiquitous on the autumn/winter 2018 catwalks, cropping up at Fendi, Dolce & Gabbana and Emporio Armani. Basically, it's a forever-wear kind of accessory.

Bellocqimages/Bauer-Griffin//Getty Images

Jenner opted for a traditional style of cowboy boot - a low-key, mid-height version in a black and burgundy mix, with a square toe and low heel. Comfortable and wearable (ideal if you're not quite ready to embrace heels again this side of multiple lockdowns), it's also proof that the boots work year-round - even in the Los Angeles spring sunshine.

The model paired her boots with a slinky black slip dress by St Agni that tied at the back and featured a slit on either side of the skirt. She finished her look with a black silk face mask, tortoiseshell sunglasses and a minimalist '90s-inspired shoulder bag.

The slip dress is another item that's managed to achieve 'staple' status in recent years, thanks to its ability to be dressed up or down effortlessly. When the style was first introduced as an underwear-as-outerwear style in the Nineties, it was considered far more risqué - and certainly not appropriate as daywear. Now, however, those lines between casualwear and eveningwear are becoming increasingly blurred, with shoppers keen to invest in pieces that work for a multitude of occasions.
