7 Top Fitness Instructors Share Their Off-Duty Workouts

July 29, 2024
blog

I practice yoga about four to five times per week, and typically mix up a few different styles. Yoga has an unexplainable offering that no other physical practice has given me before. And if it's done with attention to alignment, it can be a practice for a lifetime. I often do a vinyasa yoga practice and end with a restorative, cooling practice. Although some days I only have time for a forward fold, triangle pose, pigeon and a headstand.

The other workout I do regularly is The Class with Taryn Toomey, which I am now teaching when I'm in Los Angeles. It was a revelation to me. I have been doing yoga consistently since I was 18 and am hyper-flexible, so it's really important for me to focus on building strength and stability in my body to balance that. The Class is a bootcamp-style spiritual catharsis that combines a lot of inner emotional work (we scream and grunt!) with plyometrics and classic moves like jumping jacks and burpees. I do that about three to four times per week.

I also go through a running phase. When I'm traveling and can find a good running path, it's an easy thing to do for cardio. I recently took Nicole Winhoffer's dance cardio class and it was spectacular—she makes you work hard while still having so much fun. I also love SLT and Pilates Platinum when you need a little slow burn and some zeroing in on the abs. I also meditate pretty regularly. I don't have a strict regimen like I used to, but I will sit with myself in the morning at some point, or before bed.

I love a smoothie with spirulina, bee pollen, coconut milk or hemp milk, blueberries, pineapple and a banana after a good workout. I eat lots of greens or drink green juice. And water, of course. Coconut water or aloe water from the Juice Press are always good. And I am a big believer in apple cider vinegar and lemon water in the morning.
