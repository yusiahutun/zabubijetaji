Milla Jovovich and husband Paul W. S. Anderson don hard hats as they take a look at a huge new house

September 01, 2024
blog

Milla Jovovich and her husband Paul W. S. Anderson were seen house hunting in Los Angeles on Tuesday.

The actress, 47, and the director, 58, donned hard hats as they were given a tour of a huge new home in the upscale neighbourhood of Brentwood. 

The couple dressed casually for the outing as they strolled around the property that was still under construction. 

Milla wore a loose fitted grey T-shirt with a scooped neckline, that she tucked into a pair of light blue jeans and topped off her look with flip flops. 

Meanwhile, Paul also opted for a grey T-shirt and jeans and shielded his eyes with sunglasses.  

Busy day: Milla Jovovich, 47, and her husband Paul W. S. Anderson, 58, were seen house hunting in Los Angeles on Tuesday

Milla and husband Paul W. S. Anderson, 58, have daughters Ever, 15, Dashiel, eight, and three-year-old Osian.

The Resident Evil franchise star was previously married to actor Shawn Andrews and French director Luc Besson.

Ever is following in her mom's footsteps as a model and actress.

She's modeled for Miu Miu campaigns and is a brand ambassador for the fashion house.

Ever portrayed a young Natasha Romanoff in the 2021 film Black Widow and Wendy Darling in the 2023 film Peter Pan & Wendy.

Milla and Anderson met when he was her director on Resident Evil in 2002.

Their first child was born five years later on November 3, 2007. The Return to the Blue Lagoon star had a difficult delivery during Ever's birth.

'I was in labor for 72 hours. I was up for 48 hours, for two nights. I started Halloween night. By the time I went into the hospital, it was the night of November 2. I finally went into the hospital and had the epidural,' she told People in 2008.

Ever made her acting debut in Resident Evil: The Final Chapter in 2016 - which was directed by her father Paul -and she played the younger version of main character Alice, played by her mother.

Ever opened up about her role as Wendy in the new Disney+ adaptation, which also stars Jude Law as Captain Hook.

'Honestly, it was one of the best experiences of my life. It was an eight-month shoot and I loved every minute of it,' she said, adding: 'I was just so excited to play one of my childhood heroes.'

Ever also mentioned that her mother, Milla, 'has been my hero growing up because there was nothing she couldn't do or be in my eyes.'
