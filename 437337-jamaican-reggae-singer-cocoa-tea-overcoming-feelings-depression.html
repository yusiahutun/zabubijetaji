Jamaican Reggae Singer Cocoa Tea Says He Is Overcoming Feelings of Depression

June 04, 2024
blog
  • Reggae icon Cocoa Tea has released a new song to help him overcome depression and get back into his music groove
  • The veteran artiste said the moment he heard the beat, he felt that spark he had two years ago whenever he was going to the studio to make music
  • Cocoa Tea's new song is highlighting the consequences the pandemic had on people and how, despite going through it, they have still made it

Reggae veteran singer Cocoa Tea has released a song as a way of getting back into his craft and also dealing with a bout of depression that had hit him.

Cocoa Tea gets his groove back

The musician released a song dubbed Weh Ya Gone that highlights the after-effects of the pandemic and the upbeat rhythm has left fans showing the artiste love by streaming his music.

Cocoa's new song was reviewed on Jamaica Gleaner and they credited the veteran star for getting his mojo back after staying in the cold for a while without releasing music.

His latest song has given people a personal side of him making it clear that he wanted his fans to relate to him in the groovy song each time they listen to it.

According to Cocoa Tea, this song has made him feel good about his music especially after 'being out if' as he described it.

“It’s been a while since I’ve even felt like doing a song because we were so depressed, and all of those things just get to me, and I was out of it for a while. This song has awoken the fire inside of me, and I’m ready again," he added.

He also said that he had producers sending him different rhythms but he could not relate to them as to him rhythms are things that should make one happy so that they can make a happy song.

Cocoa gave props to Bobby Konders who sent the beat of the song adding that he knew that was the rhythm he wanted as soon as he heard it.

"I didn’t hesitate because when you hear it, you just want to party and dance. He even asked how much I would charge him, and I told him I don't need anything to sing using his beat because I liked how it made me feel like i felt the past two years. I needed something to cheer my spirit," he further said.
