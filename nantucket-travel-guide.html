The First Timer's Guide To A Chic Weekend in Nantucket

May 14, 2024
blog

Summertime in New York City is all about the weekend getaway. Whether it's the Hamptons, a preppy New England town, or the beaches of New Jersey, there are plenty of easy go-to spots to choose from when escaping the city. But most overlooked for a long weekend trip is the picture-perfect, All-American island of Nantucket.

As a New Yorker who's pretty much clueless on all things preppy and New England, I'd never been to Nantucket, Cape Cod, or any of the Northeast's quaint beach towns. So last weekend, I took a long-overdue trip to the charming island to see what ACK (as the locals call it) was all about. From the best lobster rolls to the most picturesque lighthouses, here's a guide on how to navigate Nantucket.

GETTING THERE

1) By Plane The trickiest part about traveling to Nantucket is getting there (it is an island, after all). The quickest and easiest way is to fly there. If you're coming from New York, Jet Blue offers direct flights that will get you there in the same amount of time it takes to commute from Brooklyn to Manhattan (seriously, it's an hour flight). Direct flights are also offered into the Nantucket Memorial Airport from Newark, Boston, and a few other New England spots. If you're visiting during peak summer season, however, airfare can be pretty steep. For a more private way of flying (with no TSA line), Tradewind Aviation also offers flights directly to Nantucket. Pro tip: the island is way less crowded and more affordable to get to via air in September and October.

2) By Ferry If you have more time to kill and want to go the scenic route, take the Seastreak Ferry. A six-hour ride that departs from East 35th Street in New York as well as several stops in New Jersey, the ferry includes plush seating, satellite TVs, and a full bar (not a bad way to spend 6 hours on a picturesque boat ride). A roundtrip ticket costs $240.

3) By Helicopter If you really want to make it a luxe weekend, Blade offers helicopter rides from New York and Boston to Nantucket. While this option only takes an hour and a half long, it will set you back a cool $800 or $900.

WHERE TO STAY

There are cozy hydrangea-lined bed-and-breakfast options and the newly-opened Greydon House to check out in Downtown Nantucket, along with the historic waterfront located inn, The Wauwinet. But for my first trip to the island, I opted to stay at the island's White Elephant Hotels, a luxury waterfront spot with charming private cottages and residences. There are two White Elephant properties to choose from (both within a two-block radius of each other and by the water), but I decided to stay at White Elephant Village, which is a little more modern in design and boasts one of the island's few pools.

Courtesy White Elephant Hotel

The pool at White Elephant Village.

If you (like me), are interested in sipping Bloody Mary's by the pool, this is the spot for you.

For a first-time visit to Nantucket, the location of White Elephant is ideal since it's within walking distance to all of the island's Downtown District and Main Street along with waterfront and beaches. The hotel also offers complimentary bicycles to all of its guests and complimentary BMWs to guests who rent suites—making it much easier to navigate the island. I was able to walk or bike ride to almost all the spots I visited during my trip.

WHERE TO EAT

The White Elephant Hotel boasts its own waterfront restaurant, Brant Point Grill. It's non-negotiable that if you're on the island, you must come here and order the Lobster Bloody Mary. Yes, it's a Bloody Mary served with a giant skewer of fresh lobster, tomato, and lemon and it's 100% necessary.

For a picturesque dinner experience, head to the bay-front Topper's at The Wauwinet Inn. If you're staying at the White Elephant, there's a complimentary boat ride you can take to dinner at Topper's, which is the perfect nighttime activity. The 50-minute excursion serves alcohol while offering up scenic views of the island's coast.

Courtesy The Wauwinet

Another spot for seafood fanatics is Cru, which boasts the island's best lobster roll and features chic Ralph Lauren-designed interiors. For unique cocktails and an Asian-inspired menu, try The Pearl—best known for their wok fried lobsters and lo mein dish. And located on Main Street is Met On Main, a more casual restaurant with a pretty al fresco seating area not to be missed.

WHAT TO DO

While there isn't exactly a bustling nightlife scene here, there are plenty of things to experience during your visit.

1) Take a bike ride to the island's historic lighthouses. I hadn't ridden a bike in over ten years, but trust me when I say: it's worth it to get on one in Nantucket. If your hotel doesn't come with complimentary bicycles, there are plenty of places around town to rent a bike for the day. Take a scenic ride to one of the island's several lighthouses: Brant Point Light, Santaky Light, and Great Point Light. Plus, don't miss all the gray cottages of the island lined with picturesque gardens and blooming hydrangeas everywhere.

Getty Images

2) Walk through the Downtown District. The downtown area of Main Street, Broad Street, and all the tiny cobblestone streets in-between boast a ton of cute cafes, boutique shops, and markets to walk through. Don't miss the Nantucket Atheneum Library, the historic white columned library in the heart of the town.

3) Visit Cisco Brewery. For a livelier social scene, head to the island's Cisco Brewery, an outdoor spot that includes something for every drinker: a distillery, Nantucket Vineyard's wines, and a brewery. With live-music, food trucks, and a bustling crowd, it makes for the perfect daytime drinking spot (and has its own free shuttle).

4) Spend a day at the beach. There are several beaches around the island: Jetties Beach, Great Point Beach, Madaket Beach, and even a Children's Beach. I opted for Jettie's Beach so I could hit up Sandbar for frozen margaritas with an oceanview (priorities). The beach bar hosts indoor and outdoor seating areas, with colorful chairs planted right in the sand so you can still sunbathe while sipping your drink of choice. It's the ideal beach day location.

5) Go on a boating excursion. If you skip out on the boat ride to Topper's for dinner, squeeze in a yacht tour before you leave. The waterfront lined with boats and yachts is practically begging for you to hop on and explore the island by sea. Try one of the harbor cruises or whale watching trips with Shearwater Excursions.

6) Get ice cream at Juice Bar. The island's most famous and coveted homemade ice cream spot, the line is never-ending every single night but absolutely worth the wait.

WHERE TO SHOP

Located throughout Nantucket's Downtown District are plenty of chic boutiques to check out. For on-trend accessories from straw hats and beach bags to breezy summer beachwear, try CJ Laing located on Centre Street.

For a mix of clothing with stylish home accessories, Milly & Grace is the cutest ivy-covered boutique to step into.

I also loved walking through Bodega, another chic home store with furniture, decor, and lot of unique finds to bring back at the perfect souvenir. And of course, there's a Polo Ralph Lauren store lined with white flowers on Main St—just to really achieve your peak preppiness before leaving the island.
