1Jack Robinson//Getty Images

Ralph Lauren was born Ralph Lifshitz in October 1939 in the Bronx, New York City. After a stint in the US army and two years studying business at The City University of New York, he became a salesman for a tie company, where, in 1967, he convinced the company's president to let him start his own line. He was just 28 years old.

Advertisement - Continue Reading Below

2New York Post Archives//Getty Images

The Ralph Lauren Corporation started in 1967 with men's ties and then sportswear the following year. By 1969, Bloomingdales sold Lauren's menswear line exclusively, giving the brand its own boutique, something the department store had never done before. In 1971, the designer launched womenswear and opened its first standalone store.
