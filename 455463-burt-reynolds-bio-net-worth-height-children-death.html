The famous actor was born on 11 February 1936 in Lansing, Ingham County, Michigan, the United States. His father, Reynolds Burton Milo, worked as chief of police of Riviera Beach, Florida, while his mother, Fern H. Reynolds, worked as a nee miller. The actor died of a heart attack on 6 September 2018 at the age of 82.

Education

The late star went to Palm Beach High School. Later on, he received a football scholarship that helped him enrol at Florida State University. He also attended Palm Beach Junior College.

Career

Burt's acting career began when his football career ended in 1954. He had aspired to build his football career, but his dream of becoming a professional footballer was cut short when he got an injury in a car accident. He made his TV debut in 1958 when he starred as Master Sergeant in the television show Flight.
