Keep Your Dog Away From Hot Tubs This Summer, Warn Vets

July 15, 2024
blog

Pet owners are being urged to keep dogs out of hot tubs this summer, as vets warn about the dangers of chlorine.

Despite being highly sought-after garden features, hot tub water can be toxic to dogs. A news story recently highlighted one Bulldog who was struggling to breathe after the strong chlorinated water caused inflammation on his lungs. While he quickly recovered, vets had to give him oxygen through a catheter in his nose to stabilise him.

"It may seem harmless to let your dog have a little cool off, especially with this spell of lovely warm weather, but hot tubs can be extremely dangerous to dogs and we'd advise owners to always keep your dog away from a hot tub and stick to a nice cool hose down instead," Dave Leicester, head of telehealth at Vets Now, told Liverpool Echo.

While all dog breeds should be kept away from hot tubs, vets urge owners should take extra care around smaller flat-faced breeds, such as Bulldogs, as they struggle more with breathing in general.

"Hot tubs are really dangerous for dogs – not only can the chlorine, bromine or other chemicals cause irritation to sensitive areas like their skin, eyes and ears, but dogs can also suffer an extreme reaction through inhaling treated hot tub water into their lungs," adds Dave.
