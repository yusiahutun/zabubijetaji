New Study Shows Having A Dog As A Child Makes You Less Likely To Suffer From Anxiety

October 02, 2024
blog

Having a dog as a child makes you less likely to suffer from anxiety in later life, a new study by Preventing Chronic Disease has revealed.

As part of the research, scientists compared two study groups of young children: 370 who were living with a dog, and 273 who weren't. They discovered that 12% of those who grew up with a pup of their own did suffer from anxiety, compared to a larger the 21% who didn't have one.

"What we actually found was children from homes with pet dogs had lower anxiety scores than children in homes with no pet dog,’ research scientist Dr Anne Gadomski explained to Marie Claire.

"Interacting with a friendly dog also reduces cortisol levels, most likely through oxytocin release, which lessens physiologic responses to stress. These hormonal effects may underlie the observed emotional and behavioural benefits of animal-assisted therapy and pet dogs," continues Anne.

The companionship of a dog in a family is great in helping to reduce stress, loneliness and even lowering your blood pressure. Dogs can help young children to become more confident in a social setting, as well as boosting their levels of self-esteem. And this research shows that they really are some of the best companions to grow up with.
