'Rock the Block' Fans Can't Agree on the Outcome of This Week's Episode

May 19, 2024
blog

Rock the Block is all about friendly competition, and this week, the teams battled for redemption in the main suites. Bryan and Sarah Baeumler (Renovation Island), Keith Bynum and Evan Thomas (Bargain Block), Lyndsay Lamb and Leslie Davis (Unsellable Houses), and Page Turner and Mitch Glew (Fix My Flip) headed upstairs to put their own design spins on the primary bedroom, bathroom, closet, and balcony. The result was four very different spaces, and Kristina Crestin from Farmhouse Fixer had the difficult task of judging.

'Rock the Block' Season 5

Lyndsay and Leslie created a spa-like bathroom complete with a sauna, a closet with floor-to-ceiling storage and a shoe spinner, and a balcony with a cold plunge pool. Kristina was impressed by the upscale feel and layered textures. She questioned the cold plunge ("not my cup of tea") and thought that the counter area in the bedroom felt more hotel-like. But she complimented the "brilliant" closet and called the bathroom a sanctuary. Kristina said the twins' space hit the "amenity mark" more than any other team.

Page and Mitch wanted to make the most of the room's waterfront view with a platform bed facing the expansive windows. They embraced the spacious bathroom and used Moroccan plaster and added archways, and they aimed for an unforgettable closet complete with a Champagne bar. The biggest surprise? An outdoor shower on the second-floor balcony. Kristina praised the bedroom and loved that the bed faced the water, plus she said that the bathroom had a true vacation feel, although she questioned the pink countertop. Her one critique: The closet didn't feel as elevated as what she saw in the other homes.

Keith and Evan continued their organic modern theme in the primary suite. They opened up the door for an unencumbered view and went over-the-top in their closet with a seating area, secret door to a laundry room, and fireplace. Kristina said their main suite was casual and inviting and praised the flow of the space. She called their closet "fairly epic" but wondered if the frill would've been better used in the bedroom. Her main hang-up seemed to be the linen closet in the bathroom.

Sarah and Bryan took a big risk by swapping the bedroom and bathroom in the layout to make better use of the square footage. They decided to splurge on the reconfiguration and add new windows and save on their balcony and smaller closet. They got high marks for the luxurious space and their use of colors and textures. Kristina thought the closet was a little small. She loved the well-appointed bathroom with its high-tech shower but questioned the faux planter.

After touring all of the homes, Kristina determined the winner of the $3,000 prize to be Page and Mitch! This is the team's second win of the season.
