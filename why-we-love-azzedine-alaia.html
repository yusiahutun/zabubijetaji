Why we love Azzedine Alaia

May 09, 2024
blog

As a retrospective of his work opens at the Palais Galleria museum in Paris, we chart the reasons why we love Tunisian-born designer Azzedine Alaia.

He’s the father of bodycon. Known as the King of Cling, Alaia popularised the skin-tight silhouette of the 1980s, celebrating women’s bodies with stretchy jersey fabrics, exposed zips and eyelet studding. Designers such as Herve Leger and Christopher Kane followed suite, but the credit for creating this risqué look will always be Alaia’s.

He loves women... and they love him.  A famed lover and champion of the opposite sex, Alaia counts supermodels Naomi Campbell, Yasmin Le Bon and Linda Evangelista as close friends, and is beloved by A-listers from Michelle Obama to Rihanna. 

He defies conventions. Even after 34 years in the fashion industry, Alaia still refuses to follow the rules. Fiercely independent to the end, despite being owned by the Richemont group, he shuns money-making It bags and perfumes, and ignores seasonal show schedules, preferring to focus on his private clients.

He’s not afraid to make enemies. Fashion loves controversy, and Alaia is not shy of it. He’s been outspoken about Karl Lagerfeld in the past (saying “I don’t like his fashion, his spirit, his attitude. It’s too much caricature”) and has an ongoing feud with American Vogue editor Anna Wintour, banning her from all of his shows (in retaliation, Wintour forbad her staff from attending). 

He’s been immortalised in film. “This is an Alaia!” Cher Horowitz chimes to her mugger in the 1995 film Clueless. “It’s like, a totally important designer.” And so Alaia entered into the pop cultural lexicon, even for those with zero interest in fashion.
