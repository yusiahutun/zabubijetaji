Woman gets 34% more matches on Tinder after digitally enhancing her cleavage to a DD

March 03, 2024
blog

If you're a woman looking for love, it seems that size really does matter after all.  

An experiment has found that a woman with DD-cup breasts got 34 per cent matches on the dating app Tinder compared with an identical profile for the same girl with an A-cup.

And a selection of the responses from her would-be suitors showed they were more likely to make a comment on her appearance in the DD-cup photos.

Researcher created two fake profiles for a 24-year-old woman called Carla, 24, from Manchester on the dating app Tinder. One showed her with A-cup cleavage (left) and in the other her breasts had been digitally enhanced to a DD-cup (right)

Tinder is a dating app that allows users to scroll through profiles of singletons of the opposite sex. 

Profiles contain up to five photos, and give the person's first name, age and location.

Researchers created two identical profiles for a woman called Carla, 24, from Manchester. 

Researchers used both the images of Carla to swipe right across the profiles of 1000 men. The dating app Tinder works by swiping right on a photo of someone you like, and left on anyone you're not interested in

They used Photoshop to enhance her cleavage from an A-cup to a DD-cup in a series of photos. 

Her main profile showed Carla wearing a plunging red bodycon dress that allowed her to flaunt her cleavage. 

She was also pictured in a blue bridesmaid's gown and in a red and black corset, with a black tutu skirt. 

Another photo showed her clutching a glass of wine while posing in a blue dress with white lace embellishment.

Researchers then indiscriminately swiped right on 1000 men's profile as both A-cup Carla, and DD-cup Carla.

And their results showed that the bustier Carla was over a third more popular.

Her profile with bigger breasts got 809 matches - 207 more than her flat chested twin.
