Amber Jaeger is an actress, author, and celebrity spouse from the United States. Amber Jaeger is known for appearing in movies such as “Hearts and Hotel Rooms” (2007), “The Three Stages of Stan” (2003), and “Take Me Home” (2011). In addition, she is widely recognized as the spouse of actor and screenwriter; Sam Jaeger.

Early Life

Amber Jaeger was born in Ohio, the United States in 1976. As of 2023, she is 47 years old and holds US citizenship. Her birth name is Amber Marie Mellott and belongs to the Caucasian ethnicity.

Very little is known related to her early childhood life, parents, and siblings.  However, she showed early interest in dancing and singing. When she was just six years old, she started taking classes in dancing and singing lessons and soon developed an interest in acting.

And, later, she started taking acting classes when she was just eight years old.

Talking about her academic background, she attended a local high school in Ohio. After completing her high school education, she enrolled at Otterbein University in Westerville, from which she graduated in 1998 with a bachelor’s degree in acting.

Professional Career

Briefly after completing her graduation, Amber Jaeger made her acting debut alongside David Garegnani in the 2003 film “The Three Stages of Stan.” The movie was produced by Ethan Black and is based on the short stories of writers Bob Vickery and Dale Chase who are known to have originated from San Francisco.

That same year, she appeared in movies such as “My Life with Morrissey” and “Advantage Hart.” She played the role of a hotel manager in the 2007 short film “Hearts and Hotel Rooms.”

During the same year, she appeared in the western thriller movie “Shiloh Falls.”

Caption: The poster of the 2010 film, Solitary (Source: IMDb)

In 2010, she starred in “Solitary,” a film about a psychotherapist and an agoraphobic woman dealing with her husband’s absence. She later went on to star along with her husband in the “Take Me Home” comedy romance film in 2013. Other films in which she has appeared include “And Then They Came for Me,” “How I Learned to Drive,” and “Proposals,” among others.

Books

Aside from acting, Jaeger is a well-known writer and has seven novels credited to her name. Her first book, “The Cold King,” came out in 2013, and has a 3.8/5 rating on the goodreads.com website. Her second book, “The Fire King,” was released just a month later.

She has written four books in her “Hemlock Bay” series, titled “Falling”, “Winter’s Dream”, and “Seasons End”, as well as two books in her “The Hemlock Bay Short Stories Series”, titled “Hunting Season: A Hemlock Bay Short Story” and “The Hemlock Bay Omnibus.”
