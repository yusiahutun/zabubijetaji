Straight hair trend 2022 - Hair straighteners at the ready

May 12, 2024
blog

When actress Anya Taylor-Joy attended a London screening of The Northman this week, we were not only wowed by her shimmering Dior mini dress, but her super-shiny, pin-straight hair.

She's not the only A-lister to be showcasing straight hair with aplomb; Sydney Sweeney wore it well for her appearance on The Ellen Show last week, while the likes of Kendall Jenner and Sophie Turner wore their hair in long, straight ponytails for the recent Vanity Fair Oscars party. Even the Duchess of Cambridge has been making a departure from her signature bouncy blow-dry by wearing her hair styled ultra-straight during the royal tour of Belize last month.

Clearly the famous faces – or their stylists' – have been taking notes from the catwalks: At the spring/summer 2022 fashion shows it was apparent that one key hair trend was set to rule this season: straight and sleek strands.

It was one of myriad Nineties and Noughties beauty comebacks (see also skinny brows and lip gloss) trending thanks to millennials' and Gen-Z’s nostalgic obsession with the era. And given that the look hit the Chanel catwalk, a significant following was guaranteed.

At Chanel’s Paris show, hairstylist Damien Boissinot gave many of the models centre-parted straight styles (as seen everywhere now). The shine was dazzling, unlike the dull, heat-damaged hair many of us were left with when we embraced the straightening trend first time around.

SAVIKO//Getty Images

Straight hair at the Chanel spring/summer 2022 show

A similar vibe was modelled at the 'Fendace' show (a collaboration between Fendi and Versace) in Milan. Industry veteran Guido Palau was behind the straight styles, which were enhanced by hairpieces for exaggerated length. With celebrity models walking on the runway – from Gigi Hadid to Naomi Campbell and Emily Ratajkowski – the look received endless airtime.

Daniele Venturelli//Getty Images

Gigi Hadid on the ’Fendace’ catwalk

Over in London, models at Mark Fast, too, wore straight hair well. Inspiration was “It-girl in the Nineties”, with Maria Kovacs styling the hair “straight with an attitude and shine” – the latter courtesy of the Schwarzkopf Professional Osis+ Sparkler Shine Spray.

Girls with curls at the show were left au natural, another big hair trend of the moment. (Incidentally, embracing one’s natural curls can be also be ascribed to a Nineties-Noughties influence – think Julia Roberts, Beyoncé and Sarah Jessica Parker in those decades.)

Stuart Wilson/BFC//Getty Images

Mark Fast spring/summer 2022

But whether your hair is naturally straight or textured, stylist Michelle Thompson tells Bazaar it’s versatility we’re craving this season. Since emerging from lockdowns, when we had few reasons to style our hair at all, the polka-straight look makes for a fresh alternative to beachy waves as a go-to ‘do.

Having downed tools, the pandemic also gave many of us a newfound interest in the quality of our hair, which is something we’ve all been keen to maintain since returning to heat-styling. “Healthy hair is now at the forefront and people will continue to take care of it," influential stylist James Pecis tells us. Luckily, "technology has really advanced in haircare", he adds. When it comes to straightening, this means premium heat protection and high-tech tools.

Dominique Charriau//Getty Images

Naomi Campbell on the Balmain SS22 catwalk

“I always opt for the GHD Bodyguard heat protector spray, which leaves the hair feeling silky smooth without a sticky residue,” Thompson says. She follows with the GHD Platinum+ styler, explaining “the irons straighten and smooth the hair without compromising the health and condition”. For on-the-go styling she packs the GHD Unplugged cordless styler which she says is “extremely handy for when I want to smooth out the hairline and add any detailing around the face”.

So, if you're ready to step into the straight hair trend again, know that it needn't come at a compromise to its condition. Plus, as Taylor-Joy et al prove, it can be a lot more luxe-looking than it was in our high-school days.
