The mother of a veteran who is running for Congress writes erotic books about service members, including sexually laden stories about SEALs like her son, and has admitted she has steamy special operator fantasies. 

Sharon Hamilton has authored dozens of erotic fictional tales about military men in remote locals with book titles such as 'Cruisin' for a SEAL' and 'SEAL My Love.' 

She is also the mother of Cameron Hamilton, a former Navy SEAL who is currently running for Virginia's Seventh Congressional District. 

Cameron served on SEAL Team Eight for four deployments, according to his campaign site. He spent 10 years total in the U.S. armed forces.

'Cameron is a Christian, a husband, and a father to three young children,' his campaign said in a statement to DailyMail.com 'He does not condone the genre in which his mother writes and he does not read her books.'

'Cameron loves his mother dearly and is proud of her achievements as a New York Times bestselling author, but her chosen genre of writing does not reflect his values.'

Sharon Hamilton has written over 80 romantic titles, most of which involve Navy SEALs

Hamilton's son, Cameron, spent 10 years serving the U.S. in the armed forces and Navy SEALs 

Many of Sharon Hamilton's books feature strong bare-chested men on the cover

Hamilton's SEAL series have devoted followers that frequently write blog posts about the novels on the authors website

The erotic author's library of written works is huge, spanning over 80 individual titles, over half of which are based on Navy SEALs' sexual escapades.

Hamilton, who is married to an Air Force veteran who served in Vietnam, has been outspoken about her erotic SEAL pipe dreams.

In a 2015 interview with USA Today, she described how she'd spend her time if she was stranded on an island with the military hero of her choosing.

'I'd definitely want a Navy SEAL, preferably one from SEAL Team VI.'

'With the million-dollar training and a sixth sense for danger, he'd not only protect me by getting and staying real close, he'd probably be a fun guy to hang around,' the author answered.

'As I always say in my books: hunky heroes always. Shirts optional. SEALs can swim, improvise from their environment, learn how to survive in extreme conditions,' she continued. 

'Oh, and one other little factoid: I've been told they tend to be on the hot and bothered side around women. Now that's just fine!'

Hamilton has written many multi-book series dedicated to erotic Navy SEAL fiction.

Cameron Hamilton served as a medic during his time fighting the global war on terror 

Sharon Hamilton has been on the New York Times Bestselling list for her romance novels

Hamilton has several individual series about erotic SEAL stories, including the Bone Frog Brotherhood and the Bad Boys of SEAL Team Three series

SEAL Brotherhood - Hamilton's first sexual SEAL series - features 11 installments about a different members of SEAL Team Three and their partners. 

Silver Team is one of Hamilton's newest series based on 'silver fox' Navy SEALs who  find new purpose as they age out of the service. 

The Bad Boys of SEAL Team Three shares stories of servicemen who had rough and tumble upbringings, but through their military service, find love and direction. 

Hamilton's Band of Bachelors series details the messy personal lives of SEALs experiencing divorce proceedings and custody battles, but who eventually find true love after working with their teammates to fix their problems. 

A portion of the proceeds from Hamilton's romantic military novels go to the Wounded Warrior Project and Navy SEAL Museum, according to her books.

In addition to her series on Navy SEALs, Hamilton has written other erotic series on vampires and angels. 

For the series not about service members, Hamilton opts to identify herself using the pen name S. Hamil. 

Hamilton also goes by the pen name Annie Carr when writing romantic novels centered on women. Hamilton has only written two novels under the Annie Carr name.
