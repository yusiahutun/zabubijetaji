Josh Taylor is one of the most successful and popular actors in the United States. Josh Taylor rose to fame after portraying the role of Chris Kositchek and Roman Brady on the American dramatic serial “Days of Our Lives.”

Early Life

Josh Taylor opened his eyes to this world on September 25, 1943, in Princeton, Illinois, the United States. Currently, he is 80 years old and he has a zodiac sign of Libra. In addition, he holds US nationality while his religious beliefs and ethical background have not been public. His birth name is Tommy Tim Taylor.

George Gall Taylor and Ragnhild “Rena” (Christensen) Taylor are the names of his parents. Meanwhile, his father was an athletic director of Chillicothe Township High School (now Illinois Valley Central High School) as well as a respected Football coach. He has an older brother Ron. When George was just two years old, his family moved to Chillicothe, Illinois.

Josh Taylor – Education

He attended Chillicothe Township High School (now Illinois Valley Central High School). He was recruited for his football abilities at Dartmouth College, where he played on two Ivy championship teams and earned a bachelor’s degree in Sociology. Additionally, he earned his Law degree from the University of Denver Law School.

Professional Career

After receiving his Juris Doctor, Josh Taylor chose to take a year off and work on the Alaska Pipeline. On his way to Alaska, he stopped in Los Angeles to see friends and was given a position as the Westwood Village Bratskeller manager.

He spent a year and a half at the celebrity restaurant and, surrounded by actors and producers, gave up a career in law to pursue acting. After the Bratskeller closed, he worked as a bartender at the Riverside Lounge in Santa Monica.

He made his acting debut,  playing a contemporary cowboy on an episode of the TV series “Barnaby Jones” in 1976. After this, he was seen in “The Six Million Dollar Man” and “The Hardy Boys.”

Later, he auditioned for the role of Chris Kositchek and played the role in the soap opera “Days of Our Lives” in the year 1977. Then, he played the lead role in the short-lived detective primetime series “Riker.” He balances his duty appearance in “Days of Our Lives” as well as “Riker” until the Riker was discontinued after five episodes.

Continued his successful acting career

Following his daytime success in the mid-1980s, he was cast as Michael Hogan, Valerie Harper’s husband on her new NBC series Close to Home. Valerie premiered in 1986 to average ratings but critical acclaim. Squabbles between the title star and the network led to Harper’s unexpected departure in 1997.

Instead of the projected cancelation, a surprising change appeared in the form of Sandy Duncan, who changed the show’s name to “The Hogan Family” in 1988.

Meanwhile, he was preparing for his last Day’s performance as Chris Kositchek. In 1988, he starred opposite Barbara Eden in the made-for-television movie, “The Secret Life of Kathy McCormick.”

His next project was the pilot for the television adaptation of “North Dallas Forty.” Between 1991 and 1993, he appeared in “Beverly Hills, 90210” as Dylan’s father, Jack McKay, on and off.
