Tenby family wants answers after daughter found hanged

June 01, 2024
blog

Michaela Haines, 25, from Tenby, Pembrokeshire, was discovered hanged in a stairwell outside the flat of a former boyfriend in the mystery tragedy

The family of a young woman found with traces of a 'date rape' drug in her body have vowed to fight to find out how she died - after a coroner criticised the police handling of her death.

Michaela Haines, 25, from Tenby, Pembrokeshire, was discovered hanged in a stairwell outside the flat of a former boyfriend in the mystery tragedy.

But her family refuse to believe 'inspirational' Miss Haines would have killed herself - and are taking legal action against police for their investigation.

Coroner Mark Layton will write to the Chief Constable over the police probe to raise criticisms including the failure of officers to obtain key evidence including CCTV from the night of Ms Haines' death.

An inquest found the levels of so-called 'date rape' drug GHB (gamma hydroxybutyrate) in her body were so high they could have caused her drowsiness.

The hearing was told toxicology tests showed Ms Haines was almost twice the drink-drive limit when she died and there were levels of GHB in her system.

However the inquest was told it was possible for the body to naturally produce GHB - and it didn't necessarily mean she had taken it.

Toxicologist Phillipa Wood said the levels of GHB had been found in blood following a request for a second test - finding 70 milligrams per millilitre of blood, a level which could cause drowsiness.

Ms Haines was found dead in a block of flats on December 23 last year and her cause of death was given as asphyxia by hanging.

Giving an open conclusion, coroner Mr Layton said: 'I conclude that the decision to treat Miss Haines' death as non-suspicious was the correct one. There is no evidence of third party involvement.

What is GHB and how can it occur naturally?

GHB is a depressant, which often induces a higher sex drive and a feeling of euphoria. 

Although the substance is best known as a date rape drug, it naturally occurs in the body, and is needed for the full functioning of the brain and central nervous system.

However, it can be dangerous when ingested in large amounts, while chemical additives from the synthetic production process also pose a risk. 

The drug is used recreationally as a sleep aid or for its euphoric effects,  and can be addictive.

'However, I do not find beyond reasonable doubt, which is the standard of proof that I must apply, that she intended to take her own life.

'No note of intention was left. She knew that her former partner would be returning to his flat. My view is that this was a cry for help. It follows that I do not record a conclusion of suicide.'

Mr Layton slammed police handling of the investigation - saying he would write to Dyfed Powys Police over their record keeping.

He said: 'This has potentially led to evidence not being obtained.

'The CCTV was preserved at the request of the police, it was downloaded for them, it is no longer available, much to the disappointment of the family.'

Barrister Peter Donnison, representing her family, said police failed to investigate if Ms Haines death was anything other than a suicide.

He said: 'There is no evidence that this is suicide other than the body being found as it was, there was no history of suicide attempts.

'There are no witnesses present saying they had spoken to her and she was thinking of doing this; on the contrary you had people saying the opposite.'

Speaking after the hearing, her family said they would continue to fight for answers into her death and are taking legal action.

'Notwithstanding our disappointment, we believe that this inquest has highlighted a number of failings in the manner in which Michaela's sudden death was investigated by the police.
