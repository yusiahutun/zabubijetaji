'Clueless' actress Stacey Dash is running for Congress

February 11, 2024
blog

Getty Images

Stacey Dash – who is best known for playing Dionne Davenport in both the film and the TV versions of Clueless – is running for Congress. The 51-year-old actress filed paperwork with the Federal Election Commission on Monday to run in California’s 44th district, The Washington Post reports.

The documents show that the actress’ campaign committee is called ‘Dash to DC’ and that she will be running as a Republican for the seat currently held by Democrat Nanette Barragan. She has also set up a website under her campaign committee name.

Although Dash is yet to release a statement on the news, she has previously mentioned that she would be interested in making a move into politics.

“A number of people online and off have suggested I run for political office. I wanted to see what my online community thinks of this ideas as I mull the possibilities. Thoughts?”

Getty Images

She also tweeted the ‘Dash to DC’ badge and later hinted that she had decided to run, saying: “Things are taking shape,” and the following day, she shared just the word, “Soon”.

“Come to my account for the straight news. Formal statements coming.” she said last night.

Dash shot to fame in 1995 when she starred in the now iconic film, Clueless and went on to appear in the TV show of the same name. Since then she has had parts in a number of films and TV shows and, more recently, has been very vocal about her political opinions on social media, including her views on gun violence and the #MeToo movement.

Dash has fairly conservative views compared to much of Hollywood and has even released a book about this entitled, There Goes My Social Life: From Clueless to Conservative.
