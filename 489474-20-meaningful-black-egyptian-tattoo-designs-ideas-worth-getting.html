Black Egyptian tattoos are a unique body art form that evokes powerful symbolism and meaning. You can use them to represent spiritual enlightenment, the cycle of life, and many other beliefs. The options are limitless if you are a fan of black Egyptian tattoo designs or want one.

20 meaningful black Egyptian tattoo designs

Below is a list of 20 black Egyptian tattoos worth considering. The list combines Egyptian tattoos for males and females with deep symbolic meanings.

1. Nefertiti tattoo

The Nefertiti tattoo symbol has its roots in ancient Egyptian art and culture. This iconic design is named after the famous queen of Egypt, Nefertiti, who was known for her beauty and power. This tattoo symbol often features the queen's iconic headdress and name written in hieroglyphs, honouring her powerful legacy.

2. Flying Ma'at

The Egyptian Flying Ma'at tattoo is an ancient symbol associated with the gods of justice and truth since at least the Old Kingdom of Egypt. This tattoo is usually drawn in the shape of a bird, often an ibis, and it is believed to represent Ma'at, the Egyptian goddess of justice and truth. The tattoo itself is said to symbolize a person's commitment to justice and truth.

3. Anubis

The Anubis is one of the most popular dark Egyptian tattoos in ancient and modern societies. The symbolism behind this design is quite distinct, associated with death, resurrection, and protection. The Anubis tattoo often depicts a large black jackal-headed figure with two wings in the Egyptian style, known as Anubis, the god of death and mummification.

4. The Wdjat Egyptian tattoo

The Wadjat is a sacred Egyptian tattoo symbolizing protection and health, often worn by royalty in ancient times. It features an eye in the centre of a teardrop-shaped design and is believed to represent the Eye of Horus.

5. Arrows

The Egyptian arrows tattoo is a symbol of protection and strength. It symbolizes warding off evil spirits and bringing luck to the wearer. The arrow is an ancient symbol in many cultures and was believed to be a powerful weapon used by gods and goddesses. It can also represent courage, determination, and direction. This tattoo is usually placed on the upper arm, shoulder or chest.

6. The Ankh

The Egyptian Ankh tattoo is one of the oldest symbols in history and was originally a hieroglyphic symbol from ancient Egypt. It is depicted as a cross with a looped handle at the top, representing the ancient Egyptian symbol of eternal life. This tattoo represents many things, such as strength, life, rebirth, and immortality.

7. The Eye of Ra

The Eye of Ra tattoo is a symbol used for centuries by the ancient Egyptians to represent their god, Ra. It has become a symbol of protection and power, often associated with the sun god. The Eye of Ra tattoo typically features an eye with an orange iris surrounded by a circle of flames in yellow or red.

8. Amun-Ra

The Egyptian Amun-Ra tattoo is an ancient symbol of protection and knowledge, often seen adorning the body of those seeking power or enlightenment. This symbol has been used for centuries to represent the sun and its power, and the god Amun-Ra is believed to bring about the power of light and truth.

9. The Pharaoh tattoo

The Pharaoh tattoo is a popular design that pays homage to the ancient civilization of Egypt. This tattoo usually features the bust or profile of an ancient Egyptian pharaoh, often with a cobra symbol or sun disk behind them. The symbolism of the tattoo can vary depending on the individual's interpretation, but it is generally associated with power, strength and wisdom.

10. Tutankhamun's death mask

Tutankhamun's death mask symbolizes the ultimate power of the Pharaohs and their reign over the ancient Egyptian kingdom. The mask was made of solid gold, inlaid with semi-precious stones, and crafted to depict the king's facial features. Its rare beauty makes it one of the ancient world's most sought-after artefacts.

11. Isis the observer

The Egyptian Isis the observer tattoo is popular due to its symbolism and meaning. The tattoo can depict an image of the goddess Isis, the ancient Egyptian deity associated with motherhood and fertility. It may also include hieroglyphics that translate to "I am that I am" or "I am all that is, was, and will be".

12. Nekhbet

Nekhbet, also known as "The Lady of Nekheb", was a vulture goddess from ancient Egypt who represented the motherland, Upper Egypt. She was usually depicted as a woman with the head of a vulture and wings outstretched across her back. Nekhbet was also the protector of Pharaohs, symbolizing strength and protection.

13. Kneeling Isis

The kneeling Isis is one of the most popular black Egyptian goddess tattoos used for centuries in many cultures worldwide. It depicts the goddess Isis, a powerful goddess in ancient Egyptian mythology.

She is normally presented as a beautiful woman adorning a headdress with horns and wings, kneeling on one knee with her hands outstretched. This pose is a sign of reverence and worship for the goddess and is thought to carry spiritual meaning.

14. The sacred beetle

The ancient Egyptians wore the sacred beetle tattoo as a sign of good luck and protection against evil. The belief is that wearing this symbol could receive divine protection from the gods. The beetle design is often incorporated into other tattoos, such as ankh symbols or hieroglyphs, to emphasize the power of protection further.

15. The sacred lotus

The sacred lotus tattoo is popular among those interested in Ancient Egyptian culture and mythology. It represents the beauty and rebirth of the soul, as the lotus was seen to be the flower of renewal. It symbolizes purity and innocence and is often associated with protection from the gods.

16. Hieroglyphics

Egyptian hieroglyphics are a unique type of tattoo that has grown in popularity within the past few years. These designs typically feature symbols, signs and characters used in Ancient Egypt to represent different words, ideas, and beliefs. Hieroglyphic tattoos are often designed with intricate details to give them a more authentic look. They can also combine with other symbols to create unique messages.

17. Scaled Anubis

The Egyptian-scaled Anubis tattoo is a unique and powerful design that pays homage to the ancient gods of Egypt. This design features Anubis, the god of the afterlife, depicted with scales to represent justice and balance. The tattoo is a symbol of protection for those who wear it and can remind them to stay true to their moral code.

18. Knuckles of the gods

The Egyptian knuckles of the gods tattoo is a popular design featuring intricate artwork that symbolizes strength, power and protection. The tattoo typically features written letters about gods. The tattoo honours the gods of Egypt and the wearer's strength and connection to them.

The stylized Egyptian-themed dog tattoo is a popular choice for those looking to make a statement with ink. This style combines the classic imagery of ancient Egypt, such as hieroglyphs, with the modern look of a dog.
