Gary Wilson bags magnificent 147 during Welsh Open semi-final against John Higgins

July 29, 2024
blog

Gary Wilson hit a superb maximum break to take control of his Welsh Open semi-final against John Higgins.

After taking the first frame with a century break, the 38-year-old conjured up the fifth 147 of his career to go 2-0 up. The Tyneside Terror was actually on course for a maximum in that first frame, only to miss the 14th red on 104.

However, he more than made up for it in the second, sinking several tough reds and then the colours to send the audience in Llandudno wild. It was the 199th 147 break in snooker history.

A number of fans branded Wilson a "genius" while one said: "Fantastic stuff from Gary, 2 semis, two tales, that's what is fantastic about the game. Gary on fire." Another said: "Superb, watched it, not a positional shot out of place."

Red-hot Wilson also dominated the next to make 3-0 with an incredible 333 unanswered points over the first three frames. The Wilson onslaught continued in the fourth frame, which he again took with ease.


The winner of the first-to-six affair will face Martin O'Donnell in Sunday's final. O'Donnell overcame Elliot Slessor in their semi-final following a final-frame decider. From 5-3 down, Slessor won two frames on the spin, but O'Donnell held his nerve in the 11th frame. Despite running his opponent close, Slessor slammed his own performance.

“I was useless,” he told Eurosport. “He deserved his win. I could not find the top cushion all day and missed silly balls. He made a good break at the end.

“I thought I found a gear to go 5-5 and all I was looking for was a chance, I thought I was playing a decent safety and I put the red over the hole. To be fair, he made a great break so good luck to him.”

It was the third time Slessor had come up short in semi-finals. He added: "I have lost a couple of these close semis now and have not really played great. I had more than enough chances and I did not take them and Martin deserved his win.

“I have played some decent stuff during the week, there are some positives, but I am just distraught with how I played today.”
