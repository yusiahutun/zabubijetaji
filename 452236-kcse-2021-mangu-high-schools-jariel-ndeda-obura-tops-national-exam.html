KCSE 2021: Mang'u High School's Jariel Ndeda Obura Tops National Exam

The Kenya Certificate of Secondary Education (KCSE) examination results for the year 2021 are finally out.

In the results released by Education Cabinet Secretary George Magoha on Saturday, April 10, Jariel Ndeda Obura from Mang'u High School was announced as the top student with a mean score of 87.167.

Alliance High School's Mukuha Timothy followed him with a mean score of 87.139.

Job Ngara from Mang'u High School was ranked third with a mean grade of 87.116.

Chege David Kamau from St Joseph's Boys High School, Kitale, was ranked fourth with a mean grade of 87.104 while Ramadhan Musa Tepo from Light Academy closed the best top five with a mean grade of 87.103.

Jariel Ndeda Obura speaks about his performance

Speaking to Citizen TV, Obura said he had always put a lot of effort into his studies.

"When I heard I was Kenya One I was over joyous. I did not think I will top the class. I always did a lot of practice in all the subjects and balanced them well, so I could pass all the subjects," he said.

Obura also indicated that the competition was stiff.

"I would like to thank my friends; they always told me I was going to be Kenya One. I did not always top the class, from time to time but I was in the top 10 (in class)," he added.

He also revealed that he would pursue medicine at the university.

"I will just do medicine; continue with great heights in the field of medicine, make a few discoveries," he said adding that he took Maseno University as his first choice, so he would like to join the Nyanza-based institution.

Magoha also said that candidates could access the 2021 KCSE exams results by sending their index number to 20076.

George Magoha's order on KCPE results

TUKO.co.ke previously reported that Magoha ordered school heads in public schools to give out Kenya Certificate of Primary Education (KCPE) results to all candidates.

Magoha said that public schools are free; hence headteachers should not deny candidate results, adding that in case of any issues, the school should deal with the parents later.

The CS also told the private school to give the candidate their results as they will use the result as a requirement to join high school.
